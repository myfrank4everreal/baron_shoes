Air Jordan breeze

the air Jordan breeze now the air Jordan 3 is not only one of the preeminent Jordan's of all time they're also just one of the most historic sneakers of all time thanks to them being the first basketball shoe ever with a visible air unit the trees also introduced us to other seminal air Jordan features such as elephant print mars Blackmon and of course the iconic Jump man logo which marked the first time we saw the jump man on an Air Jordan sneaker originally released in 1987 MJ wore the three's while winning the dunk contest in 88 which helped create a mystique around his shoes as sources of powers that help you take flight just like Mike the trees have also lived on as one of the more revered Air Jordan's thanks to their classic look which at the time in 1987 was one of the more unique sneakers on the market thanks to their distinguishable look and untraditional colorways like the black cement which helped the tree standout on and off of the court

while Jordan Brand has kept the sneaker alive with a ton of retro releases of the original colorways the new colorways that Jordan Brand has come up with have been a hit or miss with some pleasing fans such as the mochas the Black Cats and my personal favorite the flips the other colorways like the Joker's and the powder blues have been seen sitting on shelves leaving fans with the feeling the trees are best suited when dressed in the og colorways that MJ wore even though the three is are iconic for so many reasons they do kind of have the stigma of being your dad's favorite J's which isn't necessarily a bad thing but due to the lackluster amount of interesting new colorways compared to some of the other silhouettes in the series as well as the fact that the successor of the threes is often regarded as a superior version. forced me to put these at the number 4 spot but if you're new to collecting Jordan's or just sneakers in general a fresh pair of three is a great place to start and every sneakerhead should have a pair of these in their collection.
